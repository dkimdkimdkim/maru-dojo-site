Ray sensei began training in aikido in 1988 at the Pasadena Aikikai, Los Angeles County. Pasadena Aikikai is the 2nd or 3rd oldest aikido dojo in the continental United States. Pasadena Aikikai is located in the Pasadena Japanese Cultural Center. It is a non-profit organization.

During college, Ray sensei cross-trained in aikido and Shotokan Karate and competed in tournaments.

Sometime in 1994, Francis Takahashi sensei (7th degree aikido black belt) awarded Ray his shodan (1st degree black belt). He then became one of the assistant instructors at the Pasadena Aikikai. Approximately,four years later, Takahashi Sensei awarded him his nidan (2nd degree black belt).

Ten years later, Ray sensei began training in Shorinji-Kempo. He attained a black belt level.

In January 2005, Yoshimitsu Yamada (8th degree aikido black belt) sensei awarded Ray his sandan (3rd degree black belt). Yamada sensei was one of O'Sensei's direct aikido students. Later in the same year, Ray sensei became the chief instructor of Pasadena Aikikai.

He also began an annual aikido seminar, Pasadena Aikikai's Friendship Seminar, which is held in December of each year.

The concept behind Pasadena Aikikai's Friendship Seminar is to invite various aikido chief instructors whose aikido styles are heavily influenced by different aikido masters.

By 2008, Ray sensei shifted gears which would eventually take him to a different direction in his martial arts journey. He began his formal training in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) at Marin Mixed Martial Arts. He also began to explore the application of aikido techniques in the BJJ. Indeed, it is a rewarding work-in-progress.

With his continuing dedication to aikido, Ray sensei was promoted to godan 5th degree black belt).

In 2011, Ray sensei reached another fork on the road. This time, the path led him to train with the founder and Grand Master (GM) Robert Castro of Eskabo Daan, a Filipino Martial Arts (FMA).

In 2013, Ray sensei opened Maru Dojo, where at least 3 different distinct style of martial arts are offered. Additionally, a Judo class is offered at Maru Dojo.

Ray Feliciano is the owner/operator of Maru Dojo. He continues to maintain his role as the chief instructor of Pasadena Aikikai (pasadenaaikido.com) in Los Angeles County.
